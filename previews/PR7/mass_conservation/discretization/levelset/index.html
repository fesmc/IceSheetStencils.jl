2-D level-set method for ice-sheet mass conservation

The package supplies symbolic stencil derivations for the two equations introduced on the theory page:

  • the depth-integrated thickness equation for $H$ (in two variants — first-order upwind and WENO5), and
  • the 2-D margin level-set equation for $\varphi$, with a Godunov upwind for the advective term and an Osher–Sethian formula for the calving (Hamilton–Jacobi) term.

Both equations are discretised on the same Arakawa C-grid layout used by derive_x_residual and derive_y_residual: scalar fields ($H$, $\varphi$, $\dot M_s$, $\dot M_b$, $c$) live at cell centres, while velocity unknowns sit on cell faces — $\bar u$ on east faces, $\bar v$ on north faces.

H, φ, and the source fields live at the cell centres ●. The existing SSA solver produces exactly this velocity layout, so the mass-conservation step consumes its output directly.

Thickness equation — first-order upwind

Discretising $\partial H / \partial t + \nabla\!\cdot(\bar{\mathbf{u}}\,H) = \dot M_s - \dot M_b$ in flux form on the C-grid gives, at cell $(i,j)$,

\[\frac{\partial H_{i,j}}{\partial t} \;=\; - \frac{F^x_{i+1/2,j} - F^x_{i-1/2,j}}{\Delta x} - \frac{F^y_{i,j+1/2} - F^y_{i,j-1/2}}{\Delta y} + \dot M_{s,i,j} - \dot M_{b,i,j}.\]

The face fluxes use an upwind selection on the face velocity:

\[F^x_{i+1/2,j} \;=\; \begin{cases} \bar u_{i+1/2,j}\,H_{i,j} & \bar u_{i+1/2,j} \ge 0 \\ \bar u_{i+1/2,j}\,H_{i+1,j} & \bar u_{i+1/2,j} < 0 \end{cases}\]

and analogously for $F^y$. This is the formulation produced by derive_thickness_residual: a 5-point cross stencil on $H$, the four surrounding face velocities, and the two source fields at the cell centre.

Thickness equation — WENO5

For smoother solutions and lower numerical diffusion, the same flux form is used with a fifth-order WENO reconstruction of $H$ on each face:

\[H^{\mathrm{WENO5}}_{i+1/2,j} \;=\; \omega_0\,p_0(H) + \omega_1\,p_1(H) + \omega_2\,p_2(H),\]

where $p_k$ are three candidate third-order stencils and $\omega_k$ are nonlinear weights driven by smoothness indicators $\beta_k$. The upwind side ($i-2 \dots i+2$ for $\bar u \ge 0$, $i-1 \dots i+3$ for $\bar u < 0$) is selected by an ifelse on $\bar u_{i+1/2,j}$. The full symbolic expression is large but mechanical to derive — exactly the kind of expression Symbolics.jl is designed to handle. derive_thickness_residual_weno5 returns the corresponding Num and a compiled in-place RHS function.

Level-set equation — Godunov upwind plus calving

The margin equation $\partial \varphi / \partial t + \bar{\mathbf{u}}\!\cdot\!\nabla\varphi = c\,|\nabla\varphi|$ is discretised at each cell centre using the four one-sided gradients

\[D^{-x}\varphi_{i,j} = \frac{\varphi_{i,j} - \varphi_{i-1,j}}{\Delta x},\quad D^{+x}\varphi_{i,j} = \frac{\varphi_{i+1,j} - \varphi_{i,j}}{\Delta x},\]

(and analogously in $y$). The advective term is upwinded on the sign of the local cell-centred velocity $\bar u$, $\bar v$ (averaged from the two flanking C-grid faces):

\[\bar{\mathbf{u}} \!\cdot\! \nabla\varphi \;\approx\; \max(\bar u, 0)\,D^{-x}\varphi + \min(\bar u, 0)\,D^{+x}\varphi + \max(\bar v, 0)\,D^{-y}\varphi + \min(\bar v, 0)\,D^{+y}\varphi.\]

The calving term uses the Osher–Sethian Godunov formula for an inward-propagating front (normal speed $-c$, $c \ge 0$):

\[|\nabla\varphi|^{\mathrm{God}}_{i,j} \;=\; \sqrt{\; \bigl(\min(D^{-x}\varphi,0)\bigr)^2 + \bigl(\max(D^{+x}\varphi,0)\bigr)^2 + \bigl(\min(D^{-y}\varphi,0)\bigr)^2 + \bigl(\max(D^{+y}\varphi,0)\bigr)^2 \;}.\]

Together,

\[\frac{\partial \varphi_{i,j}}{\partial t} \;=\; - \bigl[\bar{\mathbf{u}}\!\cdot\!\nabla\varphi\bigr]_{i,j} + c_{i,j}\,|\nabla\varphi|^{\mathrm{God}}_{i,j}.\]

derive_levelset_residual assembles this expression symbolically and returns it together with the offset metadata that identifies which cells the stencil reads from.

The first-order and WENO5 thickness stencils share the same metadata shape — only the symbolic flux differs — so they plug into the same assembly loop with no other changes. The level-set stencil exposes the same offset-list interface but operates on its own scalar field $\varphi$.

Reinitialisation

Reinitialisation of $\varphi$ to a signed-distance function is done by iterating

\[\frac{\partial \varphi}{\partial \tau} + \mathrm{sign}(\varphi_0)\bigl(|\nabla\varphi|_{\mathrm{God}} - 1\bigr) = 0\]

to steady state in pseudo-time $\tau$. The same Godunov $|\nabla\varphi|$ formula above is used (with the sign of $\mathrm{sign}(\varphi_0)$ selecting the appropriate one-sided gradients). A driver-level implementation is provided in examples/levelset_margin_demo.jl; it uses the same symbolic $|\nabla\varphi|$ expression that derive_levelset_residual produces, ensuring that reinit and advection are consistent by construction.
